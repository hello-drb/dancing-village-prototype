The beginning

I've always been drawn to the deal that makes the mission possible.

Early in my career, I discovered something that's never stopped being true: the organizations doing the most important work in the world have the mission, the people, and the idea. What they're missing is someone who knows how to find the right corporate partner — and get them to yes.

That work, the finding and the persuading and the closing, became my calling.

National Geographic · 13 years

Where I learned to close deals that actually meant something.

I spent 13 years at National Geographic — growing from Director to Vice President of Corporate Partnerships. My job was to find the right companies, build the relationships, make the case, and bring in the revenue. I worked with brands, foundations, media partners, and cultural institutions across dozens of major initiatives.

What those 13 years gave me was rare: I know how corporate partners think, what moves them, and what makes them say yes — because I spent over a decade doing the persuading. I know which conversations to have early, which objections are real and which aren't, and how to structure a deal so it holds.

"She always strives to create the most meaningful partnerships — usually far ahead of their time."

The partnerships I built at Nat Geo weren't transactional. They were strategic, long-term relationships between brands and mission — the kind that hold up under scrutiny and generate real value on both sides.

Tribeca Festival · SVP Partnerships

Bringing corporate partners into one of culture's biggest stages.

After Nat Geo, I joined Tribeca Festival as Senior Vice President of Partnerships. Tribeca sits at the intersection of film, media, technology, and social impact — and attracting the right corporate partners to that stage required a particular kind of sophistication. You have to hold a creative vision with one hand and a revenue strategy with the other, without letting either one drop.

That experience sharpened everything about how I work. It reinforced that the best partnerships aren't just financial arrangements — they're collaborations that create something neither party could have built alone, and that's exactly what I pitch when I'm in the room.

The founder chapter

I didn't just sell partnerships. I also needed them.

While building my corporate career, I co-founded Rivertowns Village Green, a 501(c)(3), and created The Tarrytown and Sleepy Hollow Farmers Market — recently voted the #1 farmers market in New York State.

This wasn't a side project. It was a full nonprofit operation: building a board, navigating community politics, designing programming, finding corporate sponsors, writing grants, and rallying community support — all while serving real people with food assistance, education, and cultural events.

Running an organization that needed partners and funding — while also being someone who spent decades finding partners and funding for others — gave me a perspective that's genuinely rare. I understand the urgency on both ends. That shapes everything about how I work with clients.

Why Dancing Village

In 2023, I decided to do this work full time for the organizations that need it.

After 25 years building corporate partnership programs at world-class organizations, I started Dancing Village Partners so I could bring that same work — finding the right corporate partners, making the case, closing the deal — directly to the leaders who need it most.

The name matters to me. A dancing village isn't a solo act. It's people moving together, toward something bigger than any one of them. That's what a great partnership feels like when it's working. That's what I help build.

Today I work with nonprofits, media companies, cultural institutions, and mission-led businesses — any organization with a meaningful initiative that deserves serious partners and funding behind it.

How I show up

I'm a strategic partner, not just a consultant.

I'm also a certified professional coach (iPEC), which means I bring something to this work that goes beyond strategy decks and prospect lists. I help leaders think clearly under pressure. I ask the questions that cut through the noise. I hold the bigger picture when you're in the weeds.

I show up as a true collaborator — invested in your outcomes, honest about what I see, and committed to the work for as long as it takes.

I'm most effective when you need someone who can hold strategy and execution together — who can help you think and help you do.

How I think about partnerships

A few things I believe deeply.

01

Partnerships should work for everyone at the table.

The best deals aren't the ones where one side wins. They're the ones where both sides walk away with something they couldn't have built alone — and want to do it again.

02

Mission is the strategy, not an afterthought.

Organizations with a genuine purpose have something companies can't manufacture: credibility. That's your most powerful asset. The strategy should build from there, not work around it.

03

Relationships are the real infrastructure.

Deals get signed because of relationships. My job — and my advantage — is knowing how to build them, maintain them, and activate them for you.

04

You shouldn't have to translate yourself to be understood.

I speak both languages fluently — the language of mission and the language of business. So you don't have to choose between being authentic and being effective. You can be both.
