How I Work

I don't just give
you a strategy.

Most consultants hand you a plan and leave. I stay at the table — from the first conversation about positioning all the way through to a signed partnership. Here's what that looks like.

Every engagement
is different.

I don't offer packages. I don't have a fixed methodology that I apply to every client regardless of where they are. What I offer is 25 years of pattern recognition, a deep network, and the judgment to know what your specific situation actually needs.

We always start with a conversation to figure out which of these fits your moment.

What I do

Three ways I typically work with clients.

These aren't rigid packages — they're descriptions of the kind of work I do. Most engagements blend more than one. We'll figure out the right shape together.

01 · Partnership Strategy & Advisory

Positioning You
for Growth

For organizations that need to get their house in order before going to market — or that want to make sure they're approaching the right companies in the right way.

Before you can find the right partners, you need to be able to articulate what you're offering and why a company should care. That sounds obvious — but most organizations, even sophisticated ones, struggle to do this in a way that resonates in a corporate boardroom.

I help you clarify your value proposition, identify which types of partners would invest and why, and design an approach that's grounded in how corporate decisions actually get made. Sometimes that's where we stop. Sometimes it's where we start.

This typically includes

  • Partnership audit & assessment
  • Value proposition development
  • Partner prospect mapping
  • Pitch narrative & materials
  • Go-to-market strategy

02 · Partner & Revenue Development

Co-Creating
Opportunities

For organizations ready to go to market — who need someone to find the right partners, open doors, and shape deals that actually close.

This is where I bring my network and my instincts to bear. I research and identify the right corporate prospects, build and activate relationships, and work with you to develop partnership proposals that are tailored — not templated.

I navigate the process on both sides of the table. Having spent years as the corporate decision-maker receiving pitches, I know what makes a company say yes. I know what language works, what concessions matter, and what red flags to avoid. That perspective is the difference between a pitch that gets filed away and one that becomes a deal.

This typically includes

  • Prospect research & outreach
  • Relationship development
  • Partner meetings & presentations
  • Proposal development
  • Negotiation support

03 · Deal Structuring & Stewardship

Seeing It
Through

For partnerships that are close to the finish line — or already signed — and need someone to make sure they deliver on what was promised for both sides.

Getting to yes is only half the work. The partnerships that last — the ones that renew and grow — are the ones where both sides feel the relationship was worth it. That requires intentional stewardship from the moment a deal is signed.

I help you architect the partnership framework: the value exchange, the deliverables, the reporting, and the relationship touch points that keep a corporate partner engaged and enthusiastic. And when things get complicated — as they sometimes do — I help you navigate it without losing the relationship.

This typically includes

  • Deal structure & term sheets
  • Partnership agreements
  • Activation planning
  • Partner reporting & stewardship
  • Renewal strategy

How it starts

What to expect
when you reach out.

1

You send a note

Tell me a little about your organization and what you're hoping to build. There's no form to fill out — just a conversation starter.

2

We have a call

A 30–45 minute conversation where I listen, ask questions, and get a sense of where you are and what you need. No pitch, no pressure.

3

I share a proposal

If it feels like a fit, I'll put together a proposal that reflects what I heard — the scope, the approach, and what we'd be working toward together.

4

We get to work

Once we agree on the shape of the engagement, we begin. Most clients say the first few weeks feel clarifying in a way they didn't expect.
